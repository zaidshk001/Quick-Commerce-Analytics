# 🚀 QuickCommerce Analytics & Profit Optimization Engine

## 📌 Project Overview

This project is a comprehensive end-to-end analytics case study on a
QuickCommerce business model.\
It simulates real-world data engineering, data cleaning, KPI design,
customer analytics, operational benchmarking, and profitability
optimization.

The objective was to transform raw multi-source data into actionable
business insights across growth, operations, merchandising, and unit
economics.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🧩 Business Context

QuickCommerce companies operate in a high-volume, low-margin environment
where:

-   Delivery speed impacts retention\
-   Discounts impact profitability\
-   Customer behavior drives long-term value\
-   Operational efficiency determines scalability

This project answers 45 structured business questions aligned to real
stakeholder requirements.

## 📊 Key Analytical Modules

### 1️⃣ Revenue & Growth Analytics

-   Revenue trend forecasting
-   Category growth analysis
-   Revenue contribution breakdown
-   Pareto (80/20) revenue concentration

### 2️⃣ Customer Analytics

-   RFM segmentation
-   Churn analysis
-   Premium impact on retention
-   Referral user performance
-   Customer Lifetime Value (Proxy)

### 3️⃣ Operational Analytics

-   SLA & delay trend analysis
-   Delivery efficiency benchmarking
-   Profitability per delivery minute
-   Store benchmarking score

### 4️⃣ Merchandising & Pricing

-   Fast vs slow-moving SKUs
-   Return rate by product
-   Discount sensitivity analysis
-   Bundling opportunity detection
-   Contribution margin by subcategory

### 5️⃣ Profit & Margin Driver Analysis

-   Margin driver regression model
-   Discount impact on profitability
-   Combination analysis of premium × size × discount

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🏆 Key Insights (Sample)

-   Top 20% of customers contribute \~80% of revenue (Pareto
    concentration).
-   Premium users exhibit significantly lower churn.
-   Large order size combined with low discount yields highest margins.
-   Operational efficiency varies significantly across stores.
-   Discount sensitivity differs by category.
